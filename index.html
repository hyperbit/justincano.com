Raspberry Pi Camcorder Dedicated Device (Summer 2014)

In order to successfully capture footage of insect eggs in remote urban and agriculture environments to look for natural predators of the Brown Marmorated Stink Bug, I created camcorder for the University of California, Riverside Entomology department using a Raspberry Pi. This low-powered, dedicated device is intended for use in the field to record video and time-lapse images over a period of at least 48 hours. All files are timestamped (using an external RTC component) and saved to a mounted USB hard drive. Documentation is not yet currently available.

Senior Design in Electrical Engineering: Learning Thermostat (Fall 2013 - Spring 2014)

Guided by Professor Ping Liang for a senior design project in Electrical Engineering, partner and I created a learning thermostat (much like the Nest) as a Raspberry Pi and Arduino embedded system, that learns the user's schedule and preferences. The Pi fetches data from the Arduino, and updates its settings according to the user's schedule. the user interface consists of a web and Android app. Our notable feature is that the system can register a device to the local network and ping for it to determine if the user is home or not.
